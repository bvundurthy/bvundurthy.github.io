Research Interests

My doctoral dissertation primarily uses tools from computational geometry and game theory to compute an optimal location for rendezvous of multiple robots in an environment cluttered with either polygonal obstacles or intelligent adversaries. I am now associated with The MathWorks Inc., where I have worked on ways to deploy deep learning algorithms on ARM devices like Raspberry Pi for various robotic applications. I am currently developing advanced examples for Vehicle Dynamics Blockset and Autonomous Driving Scenarios using an agent-based modeling approach.

During the last decade, I have had the opportunity to develop various hardware constrained algorithms and demonstrate their implementation on in-house fabricated bipedal and mobile robots. I am now looking to explore various learning paradigms in developing algorithms that simultaneously track and evolve, as opposed to the conventional algorithms that adhere to a strict (and more often a single) constraint. In addition, I have been collaborating with my prior advisor to design and fabricate soft robot hands with an aim to understand their kinematics and highlight their efficacy across multiple gripping strategies. In summary, my research interests span (but are not limited to) the following fields:

  • Computational Geometry
  • Game Theory
  • Multi-agent systems
  • Deep Learning
  • Autonomous Driving
  • Soft Robotics
