Robotic Testbeds

This page summarizes various robotic testbeds that I have developed to test the algorithms. These testbeds have also been useful in illustrating optimal strategies for various players in Pursuit-evasion games. The theoretical content can be found here.

Design and Fabrication of bipedal robots-Heterogenity in rendezvous

As part of my masters Thesis, two different prototypes of bipedal robots have been designed and fabricated for accomplishing various tasks. The first robot (Prototype-I) is built with six joints, two each at hips, knees and ankles and features dynamic stability. An illustration of the robot walking is presented alongside.

Prototype II is built with just four motors in a different configuration to ensure maximum stability when stationary. The robot mimics the motion of a toddler and is capable of climbing stairs of appropriate size even in the absence of knee joints.
