Research Area

  • Alternative passive maps for systems in Brayton-Moser form
    Energy shaping methods for designing controller suffers from pervasive dissipation. The Brayton-Moser formulation was a way to get around this problem. However, even in this framework, designing controllers leads to two chief difficulties. The first one involves solving nonlinear partial differential equations, which might be a herculean task. The second difficulty lies in the fact that the methodology requires one to find storage functions satisfying certain gradient equations. Our contribution to this theory was a method to find a new class of storage functions which attain the desired objective. These could be derived without solving nonlinear PDEs and without worrying about the constraints imposed by gradient equations. For details, please refer to the papers mentioned below.

    1. Krishna Chaitanya Kosaraju, Venkatesh Chinde, Ramkrishna Pasumarthy, Atul Kelkar and Navdeep Singh, "Differential passivity like properties for a class of nonlinear systems", Submitted to American Control Conference, Milwaukee, USA, 2018.

    2. Venkatesh Chinde, Krishna Chaitanya Kosaraju, Atul Kelkar, Ramkrishna Pasumarthy, Soumik Sarkar and Navdeep Singh, "A Passivity based Power Shaping Control of Building HVAC Systems", ASME Journal of Dynamic Systems, Measurement, and Control, 2017.

    3. Kosaraju, Krishna Chaitanya, et al. "Control using new passivity property with differentiation at both ports." Control Conference (ICC), 2017 Indian. IEEE, 2017.

    4. Venkatesh Chinde, Krishna Chaitanya Kosaraju, Atul Kelkar, Ramkrishna Pasumarthy, Soumik Sarkar and Navdeep Singh, "Building HVAC systems control using power shaping approach." American Control Conference (ACC), 2016. IEEE, 2016.


  • An application of passivity to convex optimization
    Gradient-based methods are a well-known class of mathematical routines for solving convex optimization problems. These primal-dual algorithms have much to gain from a control and dynamical systems perspective (in terms of using tools from system theory), to have a better understanding of the underlying dynamics. Specifically, the primal-dual methods closely resemble the Brayton Moser form. This observation motivated us to use Brayton Moser framework to analyze the convergence of primal-dual gradient methods. As an outcome of this work, we were able to rewrite the primal-dual method as two interconnected passive dynamical systems. Please refer to the following publications for details.

    1. Krishna Chaitanya Kosaraju, Venkatesh Chinde, Ramkrishna Pasumarthy, Atul Kelkar and Navdeep Singh, "Stability analysis of constrained optimization dynamics via passivity techniques", IEEE Control Systems Letters, 2018.


  • Brayton Moser formulation of infinite-dimensional systems
    Similar to finite dimensional systems, infinite dimensional systems do suffer from pervasive dissipation. However, the Brayton-Moser form which helped one get around pervasive dissipation in finite dimensional systems, was not extended to infinite dimensional systems. This extension to infinite dimensional systems turned out to be a non-trivial task, primarily due to boundary conditions. The series of publications (given below) outlined the complete methodology for modeling and control of infinite dimensional systems in Brayton Moser framework.

    1. Kosaraju, Krishna Chaitanya, Ramkrishna Pasumarthy, and Dimitri Jeltsema. "Modeling and boundary control of infinite dimensional systems in the Brayton Moser framework." To appear in IMA Journal of Mathematical Control and Information, 2018.

    2. Kosaraju, Krishna Chaitanya, and Ramkrishna Pasumarthy. "Power-Based Methods for Infinite-Dimensional Systems." Mathematical Control Theory I. Springer International Publishing, 2015. 277-301.

    3. Kosaraju, Krishna Chaitanya, Ramkrishna Pasumarthy, and Dimitri Jeltsema. "Alternative passive maps for infinite-dimensional systems using mixed-potential functions." IFAC-PapersOnLine 48.13 (2015): 1-6.

    4. Pasumarthy, Ramkrishna, Krishna Chaitanya Kosaraju, and Addarsh Chandrasekar. "On power balancing and stabilization for a class of infinite-dimensional systems." In the 21st International Symposium on Mathematical Theory of Networks and Systems, Groningen, The Netherlands, 2014.
